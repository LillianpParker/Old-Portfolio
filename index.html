Biography

Lillian Parker, a zookeeper turned Software Engineer graduated from West Texas A&M University with a Bachelors of Science in Wildlife Biology. With a degree and a passion for herpetofauna by her side, she began working at at an Aquarium in the reptile department, and rose through the ranks quickly until she was promoted to a Lead. From there she and her team revolutionized the department and all the herpetofauna within their care.

Wanting to increase her knowledge of husbandry for a variety of taxa, Lillian moved up to North Dakota to become part of the Dakota Zoo and helped the team there care for the variety of animals within the Dakota Zoo that included felids, canids, ursids, and a variety of exotic hoofstock.

With the pandemic having altered the world of husbandry, Lillian knew it was time to move into a more stable career, and it was a sudden realization one day while working on a personal project involving HTML and CSS that should could make a career out of it, and enjoy her job too. It wasn't long until she found General Assembly's Software Engineering Immersive Program and signed up, was accepted, and within three months and under the excellent guidance of her instructors, had developed into a junior engineer.

HerpKeeper

My second project during my Immersive Course. By using Node, Mongoose, Express, and JSX, I was able to create a fully functioning fullstack application that one could add, delete, edit, and view records of the herpetofauna in their collection. I also included information about my idea behind the site, information about myself, resources herp-keepers could use to enrich their exhibits or specimiens lives, and a livestream of the St. Augestine Alligator Farm in Augestine, FL.
